# 01_GaitAI_Healthcare_Usecase.md
## What GaitAI MobilityCare Should Solve in Healthcare

## 1. What is Gait?
Gait refers to the walking pattern of an individual. Every person has a unique gait defined by their speed, step rhythm, body posture, balance, and limb movement while walking. Because gait is deeply connected to the nervous system, muscles, and joints, changes in walking pattern often reflect underlying health conditions before they become clinically obvious.

## 2. The Current Problem
Today when a physiotherapist or doctor wants to assess how a patient walks, they simply ask the patient to walk and observe manually. This method has serious limitations:
- Subjective observation: Two doctors watching the same patient walk may interpret it differently and reach different conclusions
- No objective measurement: Terms like slight limp or slow pace have no standard meaning across clinics
- No tracking over time: There is no reliable way to compare how a patient walked in Week 1 vs Week 6 of therapy
- Not scalable: Requires a specialist to be physically present every single time
- Expensive lab equipment: Proper clinical gait labs cost lakhs of rupees and exist only in large hospitals
- Not accessible: Patients in smaller cities, elderly people at home, and rural care settings have no access to gait assessment

## 3. Who Needs This - Target Users
- Physiotherapist: Writes subjective notes, cannot prove therapy progress with data. Needs objective gait numbers to track patient improvement session by session
- Hospital and Neurologist: Parkinson's, stroke, MS patients need regular gait monitoring but lab setup is expensive. Needs quick camera-based assessment in any room without special equipment
- Rehab Clinic: Post-surgery patients go through weeks of therapy with no objective progress measurement. Needs weekly scans to track recovery and make data-backed discharge decisions
- Elderly Care Center: No systematic way to identify fall-risk residents before they actually fall. Needs regular screening of all residents to flag high risk individuals early
- Caregiver and Home Care Team: Elderly person living alone, changes in walking go unnoticed until something bad happens. Needs remote assessment from home using a simple video
- Elderly Patient: Cannot access gait labs, depends entirely on doctor's visual judgment. Needs affordable accessible screening that works anywhere

## 4. GaitAI MobilityCare Solution
GaitAI MobilityCare solves this by replacing manual observation with objective AI-powered movement intelligence. A patient walks for 15 seconds in front of any camera. The video is uploaded to the MobilityCare system which automatically analyzes the walking pattern and generates a clinical-style report. No specialist required, no expensive lab, no subjective interpretation.

### WalkScan
Converts a 15-20 second walking video into measurable gait metrics including cadence, walking speed, step symmetry, trunk sway, posture angle, and variability. Generates a downloadable clinical report with a mobility score out of 100.

### FallRisk
Uses WalkScan gait metrics to automatically classify a person's fall risk as Low, Medium, or High. Gives caregivers and clinicians an early warning before a fall actually occurs.

### RehabTrack
Runs WalkScan assessments repeatedly over time and tracks changes across sessions. Allows physiotherapists and rehab clinics to objectively measure whether a patient is improving or declining.

## 5. Why This Matters
The goal of GaitAI MobilityCare is not to replace doctors. It is to give every doctor, physiotherapist, caregiver, and elderly care center access to the same objective movement intelligence that today only exists in expensive hospital labs using nothing more than a camera and a 15-second walking video.
